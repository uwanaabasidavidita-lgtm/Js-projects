California Roll

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 2 1/2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 4 sheets nori (seaweed)
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • 8 imitation crab sticks, shredded

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the sushi rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Combine the rice and water in a rice cooker and cook according to the manufacturer's instructions.
  2. In a small saucepan, combine the rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Heat over low heat until the sugar and salt are dissolved. Remove from heat and let it cool.
  3. Once the rice is cooked, transfer it to a large bowl and gently fold in the vinegar mixture. Let the rice cool to room temperature.
  4. Place a sheet of nori on a bamboo sushi mat. Spread a thin layer of sushi rice over the nori, leaving about 1 inch at the top edge.
  5. Arrange avocado slices, cucumber julienne, and shredded imitation crab sticks along the bottom edge of the rice.
  6. Roll the sushi tightly using the bamboo mat, applying gentle pressure to shape it into a cylinder. Seal the edge with a little water.
  7. Using a sharp knife, slice the roll into bite-sized pieces. Serve with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger if desired.

Udon Noodle Soup

Ingredients:

  • 200g udon noodles
  • 4 cups dashi broth
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon mirin
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms (optional)
  • 1 cup cooked chicken or tofu (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the udon noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a pot, combine the dashi broth, soy sauce, mirin, and sugar. Bring to a simmer over medium heat.
  3. Add the sliced mushrooms and cook until they are tender, about 5 minutes. If using chicken or tofu, add it to the pot and heat through.
  4. Divide the cooked udon noodles into bowls. Ladle the hot broth over the noodles, making sure to include the mushrooms and protein if using.
  5. Garnish with sliced green onions and serve hot.

Ra men

Shoyu Ramen

Ingredients:

  • 200g ramen noodles
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon mirin
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 cup sliced pork belly or chicken (optional)
  • Soft-boiled eggs, sliced green onions, and nori for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the ramen noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a pot, heat the sesame oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger, and sauté until fragrant.
  3. Add the chicken or vegetable broth, soy sauce, and mirin to the pot. Bring to a simmer and let it cook for 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  4. If using pork belly or chicken, add it to the pot and cook until it is fully cooked through.
  5. Divide the cooked ramen noodles into bowls. Ladle the hot broth over the noodles, making sure to include the meat if using.
  6. Garnish with soft-boiled eggs, sliced green onions, and nori. Serve hot.

Mochi

Mochi

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup glutinous rice flour (mochiko)
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • Cornstarch for dusting
  • Red bean paste or other fillings (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a microwave-safe bowl, combine the glutinous rice flour, water, and sugar. Mix until smooth.
  2. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and microwave on high for 2 minutes. Stir the mixture, then microwave for another 1-2 minutes until the mochi is thick and sticky.
  3. Dust a clean surface with cornstarch. Turn the mochi out onto the surface and let it cool slightly.
  4. Divide the mochi into small portions. If desired, flatten each portion and place a small amount of red bean paste or other filling in the center. Pinch the edges together to seal.
  5. Shape the mochi into balls or desired shapes. Dust with additional cornstarch to prevent sticking. Serve at room temperature or chilled.
