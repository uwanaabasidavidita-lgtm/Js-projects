Xiaolo ngbao

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped green onions
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
  • 30-40 small dumpling wrappers

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the ground pork, chopped green onions, soy sauce, sesame oil, grated ginger, salt, and white pepper. Mix well until all ingredients are thoroughly combined.
  2. Take a dumpling wrapper and place a small spoonful of the pork filling in the center. Carefully pleat the edges of the wrapper to seal it, creating a small pouch.
  3. Repeat the process with the remaining wrappers and filling until all dumplings are made.
  4. Place the dumplings in a steamer basket lined with parchment paper or cabbage leaves to prevent sticking. Steam over boiling water for about 10-12 minutes or until the filling is cooked through.
  5. Serve the Xiaolongbao hot with a dipping sauce made of soy sauce, vinegar, and ginger if desired.

Peking Duck

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole duck (about 5-6 pounds)
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Chinese five-spice powder
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the honey, soy sauce, Chinese five-spice powder, hoisin sauce, rice vinegar, grated ginger, minced garlic, salt, and pepper to create the marinade.
  3. Rub the marinade all over the duck, making sure to coat it evenly. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes or overnight in the refrigerator for best results.
  4. Place the duck on a rack in a roasting pan and roast in the preheated oven for about 1 hour and 30 minutes, or until the skin is crispy and the meat is cooked through. Baste the duck with the pan juices every 30 minutes for extra flavor.
  5. Once cooked, let the duck rest for a few minutes before carving. Serve with thin pancakes, sliced cucumbers, and hoisin sauce for a traditional Peking Duck experience.

Dim Sum

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup glutinous rice
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup chopped peanuts (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the glutinous rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain well.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the rinsed rice, water, sugar, and coconut milk. Stir until the sugar is dissolved and the ingredients are well combined.
  3. If desired, add chopped peanuts to the mixture for added texture and flavor. Mix well.
  4. Transfer the rice mixture to a steamer basket lined with parchment paper or a clean kitchen towel to prevent sticking.
  5. Steam the rice over boiling water for about 30-40 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked and has a sticky consistency.
  6. Once cooked, let the dim sum cool slightly before serving. You can cut it into small squares or serve it in individual portions. Enjoy as a sweet treat or dessert!

Roujiamo

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound pork shoulder, cut into chunks
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Chinese five-spice powder
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4-6 small flatbreads or buns

Instructions:

  1. In a slow cooker or large pot, combine the pork shoulder, soy sauce, hoisin sauce, Chinese five-spice powder, rice vinegar, grated ginger, minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Mix well to coat the pork evenly with the marinade.
  2. Cook the pork on low heat for 6-8 hours, or until it is tender and easily shreds with a fork.
  3. Once the pork is cooked, shred it using two forks and mix it with the cooking juices to keep it moist and flavorful.
  4. Warm the flatbreads or buns according to the package instructions.
  5. To assemble the roujiamo, spoon a generous amount of the shredded pork onto the flatbread or bun, and fold it over to create a sandwich. Serve hot and enjoy!
