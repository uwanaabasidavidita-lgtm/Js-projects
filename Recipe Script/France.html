Moules Marinières

Moules Marinières

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds fresh mussels, cleaned and debearded
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 shallot, finely chopped
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and chopped shallot, and sauté until fragrant and softened.
  2. Add the cleaned mussels to the pot and pour in the white wine. Cover the pot with a lid and cook for about 5-7 minutes, or until the mussels have opened. Discard any mussels that do not open.
  3. Remove the pot from heat and stir in the chopped parsley. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Serve the moules marinières hot, with crusty bread on the side to soak up the delicious broth.

Carbonnade Flamande

Carbonnade Flamande

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds beef stew meat, cut into chunks
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 cup Belgian beer (such as a dark ale)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef chunks and brown them on all sides. Remove the beef from the pot and set aside.
  2. Add the sliced onions to the same pot and sauté until they are caramelized and golden brown. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute.
  3. Stir in the brown sugar and Dijon mustard, coating the onions evenly. Return the browned beef to the pot.
  4. Pour in the beef broth and Belgian beer, making sure to scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot for extra flavor. Add the bay leaves and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for about 2-3 hours, or until the beef is tender and the flavors have melded together.
  6. Remove the bay leaves before serving. Serve the carbonnade flamande hot, accompanied by mashed potatoes or crusty bread to soak up the rich sauce.

Boeuf Bourguignon

Boeuf Bourguignon

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds beef chuck, cut into chunks
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 slices bacon, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 cup red wine (such as Burgundy)
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef chunks and brown them on all sides. Remove the beef from the pot and set aside.
  2. Add the chopped bacon to the same pot and cook until crispy. Remove the bacon and set aside with the beef.
  3. Add the chopped onion and sliced carrots to the pot and sauté until they are softened. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute.
  4. Stir in the tomato paste, coating the vegetables evenly. Return the browned beef and cooked bacon to the pot.
  5. Pour in the red wine and beef broth, making sure to scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot for extra flavor. Add the bay leaf and thyme leaves, and season with salt and pepper.
  6. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for about 2-3 hours, or until the beef is tender and the flavors have melded together.
  7. Remove the bay leaf before serving. Serve the boeuf bourguignon hot, accompanied by mashed potatoes or crusty bread to soak up the rich sauce.

Salade Lyonnaise

Salade Lyonnaise

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups frisée lettuce, washed and torn into pieces
  • 4 slices bacon, chopped
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, cook the chopped bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove the bacon from the skillet and set aside, leaving the rendered bacon fat in the skillet.
  2. In the same skillet with the bacon fat, crack the eggs and cook them sunny-side up until the whites are set but the yolks are still runny. Remove the eggs from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the red wine vinegar, Dijon mustard, olive oil, salt, and pepper to make the dressing.
  4. In a large salad bowl, toss the frisée lettuce with the dressing until well coated. Top the salad with the crispy bacon and sunny-side-up eggs.
  5. Serve the salade lyonnaise immediately as a light meal or appetizer.

Ratatouille

Ratatouille

Ingredients:

  • 1 eggplant, diced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 tomatoes, diced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they are softened.
  2. Add the diced eggplant, zucchini, and bell pepper to the skillet. Cook for about 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Add the diced tomatoes and dried thyme to the skillet. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.
  4. Reduce the heat to low, cover the skillet, and let it simmer for about 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until all the vegetables are cooked through and the flavors have melded together.
  5. Serve the ratatouille hot as a main dish or as a side dish with crusty bread or rice.
