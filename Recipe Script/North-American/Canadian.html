Pout ine

Ingredients:

  • 4 large russet potatoes, cut into fries
  • 2 cups cheese curds
  • 2 cups beef gravy
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Spread the cut potatoes on a baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil. Season with salt and pepper. Bake for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the fries are golden and crispy.
  2. While the fries are baking, heat the beef gravy in a saucepan over medium heat until it simmers.
  3. Once the fries are done, remove them from the oven and place them on a serving plate. Immediately sprinkle the cheese curds over the hot fries so they start to melt.
  4. Pour the hot beef gravy over the fries and cheese curds, ensuring everything is well coated.
  5. Serve the poutine hot and enjoy this classic Canadian comfort food!

Tourtière

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground pork
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 package of pie crusts (or homemade)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large skillet, cook the ground pork and beef over medium heat until browned. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and cook until the onion is soft.
  3. In a small bowl, combine the breadcrumbs and milk. Let it sit for a few minutes until the breadcrumbs absorb the milk.
  4. Add the breadcrumb mixture to the meat in the skillet, along with the dried thyme, ground cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Mix well to combine all the ingredients.
  5. Roll out one of the pie crusts and fit it into a 9-inch pie dish. Fill the crust with the meat mixture.
  6. Roll out the second pie crust and place it over the filling. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges to seal. Cut a few slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven for about 45 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is cooked through.
  8. Let the tourtière cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving. Enjoy this hearty Canadian meat pie!

Tou ton

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar. Add the milk and melted butter, and mix until a soft dough forms.
  2. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead it gently for a few minutes until it becomes smooth. Divide the dough into equal portions and shape each portion into a ball.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a deep skillet or fryer to 350°F (175°C). Flatten each dough ball into a disc shape using your hands or a rolling pin.
  4. Carefully place the flattened dough discs into the hot oil and fry for about 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and cooked through.
  5. Remove the toutons from the oil and drain on paper towels. Let them cool slightly before serving.
  6. Serve the toutons warm with your choice of toppings such as maple syrup, molasses, or jam. Enjoy this traditional Newfoundland treat!

Bison Burger

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground bison
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped onion
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 hamburger buns
  • Optional toppings: lettuce, tomato, cheese, pickles, ketchup, mustard

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the ground bison, chopped onion, minced garlic, Worcestershire sauce, salt, and pepper. Mix until all ingredients are well incorporated.
  2. Divide the mixture into 4 equal portions and shape each portion into a patty.
  3. Preheat a grill or skillet over medium-high heat. Cook the bison patties for about 4-5 minutes on each side, or until they reach your desired level of doneness.
  4. Toast the hamburger buns on the grill or in a toaster until lightly browned.
  5. Assemble the burgers by placing a bison patty on each bun and adding your choice of toppings such as lettuce, tomato, cheese, pickles, ketchup, and mustard.
  6. Serve the bison burgers hot and enjoy this lean and flavorful alternative to traditional beef burgers!
