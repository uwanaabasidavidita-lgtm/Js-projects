Tama les

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups masa harina
  • 1 1/2 cups chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup lard or vegetable shortening
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup cooked and shredded chicken (or any filling of your choice)
  • 1/2 cup salsa or mole sauce (optional)
  • Dried corn husks, soaked in warm water for 30 minutes

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the masa harina, baking powder, and salt. Gradually add the chicken broth and lard, mixing until a soft dough forms. The dough should be moist but not sticky.
  2. Take a soaked corn husk and spread about 2 tablespoons of the masa dough onto the center of the husk, leaving space at the edges.
  3. Add a spoonful of the shredded chicken and a drizzle of salsa or mole sauce on top of the masa.
  4. Fold the sides of the corn husk over the filling, then fold the bottom up to create a neat package. Tie with a strip of corn husk if needed to secure it.
  5. Repeat with the remaining ingredients until all tamales are assembled.
  6. Place the tamales upright in a steamer basket and steam for about 1 to 1.5 hours, or until the masa is firm and cooked through.
  7. Remove the tamales from the steamer and let them cool slightly before unwrapping. Serve with additional salsa or mole sauce if desired.

Barbacoa Tacos

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds beef chuck roast, cut into chunks
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons chipotle peppers in adobo sauce, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • 1 tablespoon oregano
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 8 small corn tortillas
  • Optional toppings: chopped onions, cilantro, lime wedges, salsa

Instructions:

  1. In a slow cooker, combine the beef chuck roast, apple cider vinegar, beef broth, chopped chipotle peppers, cumin, oregano, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and black pepper. Stir to coat the beef evenly with the seasonings.
  2. Cover and cook on low for 8 hours or on high for 4 hours, until the beef is tender and easily shreds with a fork.
  3. Once the beef is cooked, shred it using two forks and mix it with the cooking juices to keep it moist.
  4. Warm the corn tortillas in a skillet or microwave until pliable.
  5. Assemble the tacos by placing a generous amount of shredded barbacoa on each tortilla. Top with chopped onions, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime juice if desired.
  6. Serve the barbacoa tacos with your favorite salsa on the side. Enjoy!

Gorditas

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups masa harina
  • 1 1/4 cups warm water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup cooked and shredded meat (such as chicken, beef, or pork)
  • 1/2 cup refried beans (optional)
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheese (optional)
  • Oil for frying
  • Optional toppings: salsa, sour cream, guacamole, chopped onions, cilantro

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the masa harina and salt. Gradually add the warm water, mixing with your hands until a soft dough forms. The dough should be moist but not sticky. If it's too dry, add a little more water; if it's too sticky, add a bit more masa harina.
  2. Divide the dough into equal portions and shape each portion into a ball. Flatten each ball into a thick disc using your hands or a tortilla press.
  3. If desired, spread a layer of refried beans on one half of the disc, then add a spoonful of shredded meat and sprinkle with cheese. Fold the other half of the disc over the filling and press the edges together to seal.
  4. Heat oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Fry the gorditas for about 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and cooked through.
  5. Remove the gorditas from the oil and drain on paper towels. Let them cool slightly before slicing open to reveal the filling.
  6. Serve the gorditas with your favorite toppings such as salsa, sour cream, guacamole, chopped onions, and cilantro. Enjoy!

Ceviche

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fresh fish filet (such as tilapia, snapper, or sea bass), cut into small cubes
  • 1/2 cup fresh lime juice
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup chopped red onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 jalapeño pepper, seeded and finely chopped (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Optional: diced avocado, diced tomatoes, or sliced radishes for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a glass or ceramic bowl, combine the cubed fish with the lime juice and lemon juice. Make sure the fish is fully submerged in the citrus juice. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to 1 hour, or until the fish is opaque and "cooked" by the citrus juice.
  2. Once the fish is ready, drain off most of the citrus juice, leaving a little bit to keep the ceviche moist.
  3. Add the chopped red onion, cilantro, jalapeño pepper (if using), salt, and black pepper to the bowl with the fish. Gently toss to combine all the ingredients.
  4. Let the ceviche sit for an additional 10 minutes in the refrigerator to allow the flavors to meld together.
  5. Serve the ceviche chilled, garnished with diced avocado, diced tomatoes, or sliced radishes if desired. Enjoy with tortilla chips or as a light appetizer!
